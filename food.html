Famous Local Foods

  • Nasi Lemak: Frequently referred to as the national dish of Malaysia, Nasi Lemak can be consumed at breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Its name, which in Malaysian literally means 'oily or fatty rice', is taken in this context to mean 'rich' or 'creamy', and refers to the cooking process.
  • Roti Jala: Roti Jala, or "net bread", gets its name from the lines that are created by drizzling batter on a large skillet. The final product is folded up like a crepe and usually served with a curry.
  • Nasi Kerabu: This colorful dish will both catch your eye and tantalize your taste buds. The blue rice in Nasi Kerabu gets its color from telang flowers, which are crushed and mixed into it. The rice is then topped with dried fish or fried chicken, bean sprouts, prawn crackers, cabbage, and other salads.
