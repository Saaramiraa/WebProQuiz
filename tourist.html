Top Tourist Attractions in Selangor

  • Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Mosque

    The Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Mosque, also known as the Blue Mosque, is one of Malaysia’s most iconic religious landmarks. Located in Shah Alam, it is the largest mosque in Malaysia and one of the largest in Southeast Asia. Completed in 1988, it features a massive blue and silver dome, and can accommodate up to 24,000 worshippers. Its design blends traditional Islamic architecture with modern elements, making it a cultural and spiritual gem.

  • Batu Caves

    Batu Caves is a significant cultural landmark and a symbol of Malaysia's rich diversity. Located at Gombak, Selangor, it serves as a sacred site for Hindus, especially during the Thaipusam festival. The prominent Hindu shrine here reflects the Indian community's integral role in Malaysia’s multicultural fabric, where people from all races gather to admire the caves and participate in its traditions.

  • Zoo Negara

    Zoo Negara is Malaysia's prominent wildlife sanctuary located in Ulu Klang, Selangor. Established in 1963, it spans 110 acres and houses over 5,000 animals across more than 400 species. The zoo is known for its Giant Panda Conservation Centre and plays a crucial role in wildlife conservation efforts in Malaysia.
